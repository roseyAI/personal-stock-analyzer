What is ESG?

ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance - a framework used to evaluate how sustainable and ethical a company's business practices are.

  • Environmental: How the company impacts the environment (carbon footprint, renewable energy, waste management)
  • Social: How the company treats employees, customers, and communities (diversity, labor practices, data privacy)
  • Governance: How the company is managed (board diversity, executive compensation, business ethics)

Companies with higher ESG scores are generally considered more sustainable and may be better long-term investments, though this can vary by individual priorities.
